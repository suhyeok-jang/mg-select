### 4. TL;DR (one-liner, highlighted)
> We propose a masking distribution-guided test-time scaling method to improve precision of VLAs in robotic manipulation.

### 5. Abstract (compressed)
VLAs excel at robot control but their single-inference, greedy-decoding paradigm is a bottleneck for high-precision manipulation. Existing test-time scaling methods rely on external verifiers that require extra training and fail to generalize. We propose **Masking Distribution Guided Selection (MG-Select)**, a verifier-free test-time scaling framework that uses KL divergence from a *condition-masking reference distribution* — generated by the same VLA with randomly masked states/language — as a Best-of-N confidence signal. A joint training strategy (dropout on state and language) further sharpens the reference. MG-Select consistently improves base VLAs across simulation and real-world benchmarks without any additional module.

### 7. Method (3 subsections)

**7.1 Motivation** (from Introduction)
VLAs struggle on fine-grained manipulation — millimeter accuracy matters, but greedy decoding is a bottleneck. Prior TTS work pairs sampling with an *external verifier*, which (i) needs extra RL training and (ii) fails to generalize to unseen tasks/objects. Our goal: a TTS framework that uses only the model's internal properties.

**7.2 Condition-Masking Distributional Confidence** (Section 3.2, with Figure 1)
Naive likelihood-based Best-of-N fails because fine-tuned VLAs produce overly concentrated action distributions — samples collapse. Instead, we measure KL divergence between the predicted distribution and a *reference distribution that represents uncertainty*. The reference is produced by the same VLA but with specific inputs masked (text, state, or both), approximating failure modes where essential conditions are ignored:

- Text-masking: `KL(π(·|o,q,∅,a<i) ∥ π(·|o,q,I,a<i))`
- State-masking: `KL(π(·|o,∅,I,a<i) ∥ π(·|o,q,I,a<i))`
- Both: `KL(π(·|o,∅,∅,a<i) ∥ π(·|o,q,I,a<i))`

Actions that *deviate most* from the uncertainty-aware reference are the most confident. Best-of-N: `a* = argmax C_a`.

**7.3 Joint Training Strategy** (Section 3.3)
Existing VLAs aren't trained for condition-masking inputs, so directly masking gives garbage. We augment fine-tuning with four variants per sample: all-condition, text-masking, state-masking, both-masking — each picked with a small dropout probability. The resulting VLA (denoted **MG-Select\***) maintains standard-fine-tuning performance while producing meaningful condition-masking references, giving a stronger confidence signal at test time.

### 9. Efficient Deployment (Figure 3 + Table 5(b))
Running N forward passes means N prefills — a bottleneck for VLAs that re-prefill every step. Our **single-prefill deployment** shares one prefill across all N candidates before decoding. With N=4, this gives a **45% latency reduction** vs. vanilla MG-Select, keeping inference time close to single-action across candidate sizes.

Table 5(b): performance vs. N (PnP / All). N=4 is the sweet spot — further gains beyond N=4 are marginal but latency grows.

### 10. Benchmark Results (4 benchmarks)

**10.1 RoboCasa** — 8 pick-and-place tasks, 3 demo scales (30/100/300). MG-Select* gives the largest gains in the low-data regime (+168% rel. at 30 demos on PnP).

**10.2 Real-World (Franka Research 3)** — in-distribution pick-and-place (4 objects, 24 trials each) and out-of-distribution (tape, cup) tasks. Consistent +28% / +35% gains.

**10.3 SIMPLER-WidowX** — 4 pick-and-place tasks, real-to-sim evaluation. MG-Select* improves π₀-FAST from 46.9 → 50.3 average, outperforming RT-1-X, Octo, RoboVLM, SpatialVLA on average.

**10.4 CALVIN** — Long-horizon zero-shot generalization (Appendix F, Table 15). Train π₀-FAST on environments A/B/C, zero-shot eval on novel environment D over 1,000 instruction chains across 34 tasks (Franka Panda). MG-Select* improves success at every chain depth (1–5 steps), raising average chain length from 3.69 → 3.86.
