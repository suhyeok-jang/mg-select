for Vision-Language-Action Models

Abstract

Vision-Language-Action models (VLAs) have demonstrated remarkable performance in robot control. However, they remain fundamentally limited in tasks that require high precision due to their single-inference paradigm. While test-time scaling approaches using external verifiers have shown promise, they require additional training and fail to generalize to unseen conditions.

We propose Masking Distribution Guided Selection (MG-Select), a novel test-time scaling framework for VLAs that leverages the model's internal properties without requiring additional training or external modules. Our approach utilizes KL divergence from a reference action token distribution as a confidence metric for selecting the optimal action from multiple candidates. We introduce a reference distribution generated by the same VLA but with randomly masked states and language conditions as inputs, providing action uncertainty while remaining aligned with the target task distribution.

Additionally, we propose a joint training strategy that enables the model to learn both conditional and unconditional distributions by applying dropout to state and language conditions, thereby further improving the quality of the reference distribution. Our experiments demonstrate that MG-Select provides a reliable reference for action selection through task-relevant condition masking and consistently improves base models across diverse simulation and real-world benchmarks.

Method

The Problem

VLAs fine-tuned on target tasks for next action token prediction often memorize expert trajectories, causing the probability distribution over action tokens to become overly concentrated. As a result, naive likelihood-based selection from multiple samples often converges to the same result and fails to improve precision.

Condition-Masking Distributional Confidence

We propose a confidence metric based on the KL divergence between the predicted distribution and a reference distribution that represents uncertainty. The reference is generated by the same VLA but with specific input modalities masked out — creating a distribution that approximates failure modes where essential conditions are ignored.

Text-masking: $\mathrm{KL}_{\text{text}} = \mathrm{KL}\big(\pi_\theta(\cdot \mid o_t, q_t, \varnothing, a_{<i}) \,\|\, \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid o_t, q_t, I, a_{<i})\big)$
State-masking: $\mathrm{KL}_{\text{state}} = \mathrm{KL}\big(\pi_\theta(\cdot \mid o_t, \varnothing, I, a_{<i}) \,\|\, \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid o_t, q_t, I, a_{<i})\big)$
Both: $\mathrm{KL}_{\text{both}} = \mathrm{KL}\big(\pi_\theta(\cdot \mid o_t, \varnothing, \varnothing, a_{<i}) \,\|\, \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid o_t, q_t, I, a_{<i})\big)$

Actions that deviate most from this uncertainty-aware reference are likely to be the most confident and precise. We select the final action via Best-of-N:

$a^{*} = \arg\max_{\tilde{a}^{(n)} \in \tilde{\mathcal{A}}} C_{\tilde{a}^{(n)}}$

Joint Training Strategy

To ensure the VLA can produce meaningful condition-masking distributions at inference, we propose a joint fine-tuning scheme. We randomly drop the proprioceptive state $q_t$ and/or instruction $I$ (each 10%) during training, augmenting the dataset with four variants:

(i) all-condition $(q_t, I)$ (ii) text-masking $(q_t, \varnothing)$ (iii) state-masking $(\varnothing, I)$ (iv) both-masking $(\varnothing, \varnothing)$

This enables the VLA to maintain standard fine-tuning performance while gaining awareness of condition-masking distributions — yielding a stronger confidence signal at test time. We denote this enhanced variant as MG-Select*.

Qualitative Results

MG-Select improves the precision of the policy at critical moments — grasping and releasing — where the base model often fails.

Efficient Deployment

We design a single-prefill deployment strategy that shares one prefill across all $N$ candidates before decoding. With $N=4$, this reduces inference latency by 45% compared to vanilla MG-Select — keeping the inference time comparable to single-action inference across different candidate sizes.
